BLACK-JACK

Card Values:
1. Number cards (2-10) are worth their face value.
2. Face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are each worth 10.
3. Aces can be the value of 1 or 11, depending on which value benefits the hand more.

Objective:
The main goal of Blackjack is to beat the dealer by having a hand value closer to 21 (targetValue) without exceeding it.

Game Start:
Players are dealt two cards, facing up.
The dealer receives one card facing up and one facing down.

Player's Turn:
Players can choose to "Hit" to receive additional cards.
Players can "Stand" to keep their current hand and end their turn.
Players can continue to "Hit" until they decide to "Stand" or until bust, their hand value exceeds 21 (targetValue).

Dealer's Turn:
The dealer reveals their card that is facing down (hole card).
The dealer must "Hit" until their hand value is at least 17 (dealerMaxValue).
The dealer must "Stand" if their hand value is 17 (dealerMaxValue) or higher.

Result:
Player wins: Player gains the amount of money betted
Dealer wins: Player loses the amount of money betted
Tie: -nothing happens -
